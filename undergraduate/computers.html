Computer Recommendations

Consult http://sites.udel.edu/computing-purchases/ for specific information, on recommended configurations, and special deals available to UD students. The general advice there applies equally well to Computer Science majors.

  • Several of our courses require access to a Unix operating system. This can be done remotely by installing free software to connect your computer to the central systems via an "ssh" program. If you choose a Mac you can also work locally using your own machine because Mac OSX is already a Unix operating system. If you choose a Windows machine, you can download a freeware package called "cygwin" that will give you almost all the benefits of having the Unix operating system on your machine that you get with Mac OSX. See www.cygwin.com for details.
  • Though it is not a requirement of the CS program, most students find it useful to have a copy of Microsoft Office, i.e. Word/Excel/PowerPoint. You can purchase a *legal* copy at a *major* academic discount by going through the University of Delaware bookstore: http://sites.udel.edu/computing-purchases/buy-personal/.
  • The University of Delaware Campus has a comprehensive wireless network. For this reason you may find a laptop with high-speed wireless (802.11g/n) to be sufficient and more enjoyable than a desktop in your room.
